Our Commerce wing is designed to bridge the gap between classroom theory and real-world financial literacy.

1. Book-keeping & Accountancy (The Core)
Fundamental Accounting: Introduction to Book-keeping, Double Entry System, Journalizing, and Ledger Posting.

Subsidiary Books: Cash Book, Purchase/Sales Book, and Bank Reconciliation Statements (BRS).

Final Accounts: Detailed preparation of Trading, Profit & Loss A/c, and Balance Sheets for Proprietary and Partnership firms.

Partnership Accounts: Admission, Retirement, and Death of a Partner; Dissolution of Partnership Firms.

Corporate Accounting: Issue of Shares (Forfeiture and Reissue) and Analysis of Financial Statements.

Modern Tools: Computerized Accounting Systems and Tally basics.

2. Economics (Micro & Macro)
Microeconomics: Study of Individual units, Utility Analysis, Law of Demand, Elasticity of Demand, and Supply Analysis.

Market Forms: Perfect Competition, Monopoly, and Monopolistic Competition.

Macroeconomics: National Income calculation (Output, Income, and Expenditure methods).

Public Finance: Understanding the Government Budget, Revenue vs. Capital Expenditure, and Taxation.

Financial Markets: The role of RBI, Commercial Banks, Stock Markets (BSE/NSE), and SEBI.

3. Organisation of Commerce & Management (OCM)
Management Principles: Henry Fayol’s 14 Principles and F.W. Taylor’s Scientific Management.

Functions of Management: Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing, Coordinating, and Controlling.

Business Services: Detailed study of Banking, Insurance, Transport, Warehousing, and Communication.

Social Responsibilities: Business Ethics and Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR).

Consumer Protection: Rights of consumers and the Redressal Forum (District, State, and National levels).

4. Secretarial Practice (SP) / Mathematics
Corporate Finance: Capital Structure (Fixed vs. Working Capital) and Sources of Finance (Equity, Debentures, Deposits).

Depository System: Dematerialization (Demat) and Rematerialization process.

Business Math (Optional): Mathematical Logic, Matrices, Differentiation, and Regression Analysis.
